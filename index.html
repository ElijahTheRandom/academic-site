About Me

I am an undergraduate Computer Science researcher at the University of Alabama in Huntsville focusing on trustworthy AI, multimodal detection of AI-generated content, and transformer-based generative forensics. My work spans diffusion residual analysis, segmentation pipelines, and multimodal robustness, with applications in computer vision, audio, RF, and document forensics.

Research Interests

  • Generative model forensics (DNF, diffusion inversion, multimodal IDR)
  • Transformer architectures for detection and segmentation
  • AI security, robustness, and trustworthiness
  • Multimodal AI: image, video, audio, hyperspectral, RF

Selected Projects

  • Diffusion Noise Feature Segmentation (DNF-U-Net): Localization of AI manipulations using diffusion-based residuals.
  • Inverse Diffusion Residuals (IDR): Extending residual forensic analysis to audio, video, RF, and 3D modalities.
  • 1613 King James Bible Digitization: YOLOv12 object detection + CNN letter classification for document reconstruction.
  • Drone Programming & Cybersecurity Robotics: ROS2-based control systems for multi-agent research.
