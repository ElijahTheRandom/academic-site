About Me

I am an undergraduate Computer Science researcher at the University of Alabama in Huntsville specializing in trustworthy and generative artificial intelligence, with complementary work in robotics and autonomous systems. My recent research focuses on developing physically grounded methods for detecting and localizing AI-generated or AI-altered media. I am interested in how we can analyze and generate content in ways that improve reliability, transparency, and public trust in modern generative AI systems.

I am also broadly interested in how information is transformed across modalities, and how the resulting systems can be made more reliable, transparent, and aligned with human reasoning. My recent work applies these ideas to detecting and localizing AI-generated or AI-altered media, but my long-term goal is to contribute to the core science of generative modeling and multimodal representation learning. This motivation has guided my projects in diffusion residual analysis, few-shot video frame generation, and multimodal perception for autonomous systems.

Beyond research, I founded and currently lead the UAH Data Science & Artificial Intelligence Club, a 150+ member student organization dedicated to enabling beginner-friendly AI education and fostering interdisciplinary collaboration. Through workshops, outreach, and mentorship, I work to bridge technical innovation with accessibility and ethical engagement, ensuring that advancements in AI serve both science and society.

Research Interests

  • Trustworthy and interpretable AI
  • Foundations of generative models (diffusion, autoencoders, transformers)
  • Multimodal generative modeling and representation learning
  • Generative multimodal forensics and reliability of AI-generated media
  • Cross-domain robustness and evaluation
    • Image, video, audio, and RF / other sensor data
    • Unified approaches that transfer across modalities
